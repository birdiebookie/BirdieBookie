😈 Demon

Three matching scores can be dangerous. One player becomes the Demon.

How Demon Works

Demon is built around 3 matching scores on a hole.

If three players score the same number, the remaining player becomes the Demon.

The Demon must then pay all three players.

  • 3 pars and 1 bogey = Demon pays
  • 3 bogeys and 1 double = Demon pays
  • 3 birdies and 1 par = Demon pays BIG

This creates insane pressure on short putts and recovery shots.

The Famous Demon Putt

Players have been known to intentionally miss putts to avoid becoming the Demon.

Example:

  • Three players already have bogey
  • The last player has a short bogey putt
  • Making it means becoming the Demon and paying everyone
  • Missing it avoids the penalty

That creates one of the strangest pressure moments in golf gambling.

BirdieBookie Tip

Before the round decide:

  • Demon payout amount
  • Whether birdies count double
  • If triples or worse qualify
  • Carryover rules

Demon creates nonstop tension because matching scores become dangerous instead of safe.
