🎲 Vegas

Big numbers. Big swings. Big gambling energy. Vegas is one of the wildest golf betting games ever invented.

How Vegas Works

Vegas is usually played 2 vs 2.

Each team's scores are combined into a two-digit number.

Example:

  • Player A scores 4
  • Player B scores 5
  • Team score = 45

Lower number wins the hole. The difference between team scores determines the payout.

Example: 45 vs 56 = 11-point difference.

Popular Vegas Variations

Flip the Bird

A birdie flips the opponent’s score for massive swings.

Double Vegas

Par 3s or Par 5s are worth double points.

High-Low Vegas

Higher score always goes first creating bigger numbers.

BirdieBookie Tip

Vegas can get expensive FAST. Especially when birdies flip numbers.

Before the round decide:

  • Dollar value per point
  • Birdie flip rules
  • Double holes
  • Maximum loss limits

This game creates huge momentum swings and nonstop pressure.
